#  Resources

##  1. Introduction

### Learning Radiology – William Herring
- Best entry-level radiology book
- Teaches how to read medical images
- Covers: X-ray, CT, MRI basics

---

##  2. Anatomy (Imaging-Oriented)

### Imaging Atlas of Human Anatomy – Weir & Abrahams
- Direct mapping of anatomy → medical images
- Helps understand structures in scans

---

##  3. Mammography (Core Focus)

### Breast Imaging: The Requisites – Debra Ikeda
- Focused radiology perspective
- Covers:
  - Mass vs calcification
  - BI-RADS logic
  - Clinical interpretation

### Atlas of Mammography
- Case-heavy visual learning
- Builds pattern recognition

---

##  4. MRI (Beginner → Intermediate)

### MRI Made Easy – Govind Chavhan
- Simple introduction to MRI
- Avoids heavy physics initially

### Handbook of MRI Technique
- More detailed MRI understanding
- Still practical and readable

---

##  5. Intermediate (6–12 months)

### Core Radiology – Jacob Mandell
- Resident-level resource
- Use as reference, not linear reading

### Grainger & Allison’s Diagnostic Radiology
- Advanced reference book
- Deep coverage of imaging modalities

---

##  6. Cancer & Clinical Understanding

### The Biology of Cancer – Robert Weinberg
- Fundamental cancer biology
- Explains why lesions appear as they do

---

##  7. Supplementary

### Radiology Secrets
- Q&A format
- Fast review and reinforcement
